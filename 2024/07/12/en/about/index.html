About

Jul 12 2024 2 minutes read (About 352 words)

During my study of math in the university, I developed an unfavored view of the majority of textbooks and lecture notes — to introduce a new concept, most texts start a chapter like this:

Fourier transform is defined by …*a funky equation*.

Wait. What on earth is a fourier transform? How do we arrive at this equation?

We define the potential of a positive unit charge at the origin in $R^3$ as …*a magic equation*…

And the vector field associated with this potential is …*a more misterious equation*

Wait wait wait … what does potential mean? What is a positive unit charge? (I got no sense of physics as a math student back then) What does this vector field represent?

Now you may argue that I could have filled those knowledge gaps on the Internet or talk to professors in person. I did. And that made those textbooks and lecture notes nothing more than a detailed “Table of Contents” that guided my search.

The problem is that learning is about building connections between new knowledge and those already known. So, a more natural and effective way of teaching should adhere to the following procedures:

  1. Introduce a problem that cannot be solved with our existing knowledge; and then

  2. Provide a new solution or technique to this problem; last and very least

  3. Abstract this solution and form a new concept.

If you look at those textbooks I mentioned earlier, you will find that they present the knowledge in exactly reversed order: throw out a new concept out of nowhere; give a formal deduction to this concept; show where the concept can be applied to. After reading through the chapters, I had no idea why I was in those courses in the first place.

And that leads to the theme of this blog: writing articles that do the teaching, can be understood by everyone with a high school degree, without losing depth in knowledge. While this sounds pretty ambitious and may not be achieved so easily, it is a concrete step that breaks out of the academic ivory tower. Without rambling any further, let’s go!
